Technology Overview

High-Speed Video Capture

Footage of machinery is captured at high frame rates to expose subtle, invisible vibrations. Using OpenCV, each video is processed frame-by-frame as numerical arrays for further analysis.

Eulerian Motion Magnification

Subtle motions are amplified by enhancing pixel intensity changes over time. A temporal bandpass filter isolates relevant frequency ranges, and magnification factors highlight meaningful vibrations without distortion.

Signal Extraction

Intensity changes over time are tracked from individual pixels or regions. The result is a 1D time-series signal that reflects micro-vibrations with high temporal precision.

Fourier Transform (FFT)

Signals are transformed into the frequency domain using FFT to expose dominant vibration frequencies. We compute magnitude or power spectra and extract features like peak frequency and spectral entropy.

AI-Powered Inference

Extracted features are fed into machine learning models to classify faults and detect anomalies. Depending on dataset quality, we use statistical inputs or raw spectra to drive predictive insights.
